Three B2B SaaS earnings prints in the past two weeks told the same story in different shapes. HubSpot Q1: active core seat users up 90% year over year, credit consumption up 67% quarter over quarter. ServiceNow Q1: $1M-plus Now Assist accounts up 130%, with the 2026 Now Assist ACV target raised on the call from $1B to $1.5B. Klaviyo Q1: revenue up 28% on the launch of two agent SKUs priced separately from the marketing seat. Different products. One move.

The seat license is becoming the base fee. The credit line is becoming the renewal conversation. The procurement team that asked "how many users on the contract" a year ago is now asking "how many credits per active user, growing." For builders, the next twelve months of pricing strategy turn on a single question: does the AI feature in your product have a meter the buyer can read and a value the buyer can defend. Both, or neither.

## What's actually moving this week

**1. ServiceNow raised the Now Assist target mid-year.** Q1 FY26 subscription revenue $3.67B, up 22%. The signal wasn't the top line. Bill McDermott bumped the full-year Now Assist ACV target from $1B to $1.5B on the May earnings call. Customers spending $1M-plus on Now Assist grew 130% year over year. Deals with three or more Now Assist products grew nearly 70%. EmployeeWorks — the rebranded Moveworks-plus-employee-portal — closed more deals in Q1 alone than Moveworks closed in all of 2025. If you sell into the IT or HR ops seat, your renewal champion just got a Now Assist consumption chart pinned to their procurement deck.

**2. HubSpot credit consumption outran seat growth.** Q1 2026 revenue $881M, up 23%. Active core seat users up 90% year over year. Credit consumption up 67% quarter over quarter — outpacing seat growth on a faster clock. Customer Agent, Prospecting Agent, and Data Agent each priced on credits, not per seat. The CFO commentary was unambiguous: the credit line is the leading indicator, the seat line is trailing. If your AI feature still ships behind a per-seat license, the analyst comparison sheet has you in the wrong column by Q3.

**3. Klaviyo opened agent SKUs as a separate column on the invoice.** Q1 revenue $358M, up 28%. NRR back to 110%. Composer (campaign creation) and Customer Agent shipped this quarter on consumption pricing, sold next to the marketing seat rather than inside it. The marketing-automation buyer now reads two columns — seat and agent — and the agent column is the growth story the CFO talks about on the call.

**4. Adobe's AI-first ARR tripled year over year.** Q1 FY26 revenue $6.4B, up 12%. AI-first ending ARR more than 3x. Firefly Enterprise new logos up 50%. Firefly Foundry — private-model training on the customer's own IP — is now a paid SKU. The "AI inside the renewal" pattern has left B2B SaaS and entered creative tooling. Every category gets this column on the price book by year-end.

## What I'd ship in your app this week

**Feature one: the credit meter, on every AI action.** The user fires an AI feature — generates a campaign summary, runs a natural-language query against the semantic layer, asks the agent to draft an outreach sequence. Underneath the result, a small chip: *0.4 credits — $0.012.* Hover to see the breakdown: which retrieval ran, which model answered, the token count, the cached portion. The buyer's admin gets a weekly roll-up by team and by feature, exportable to the renewal-procurement deck. The meter is the moat. The first vendor in any category to ship a meter the buyer can defend wins the next renewal cycle.

<ul class="list-bullet">
  <li><strong>Shape.</strong> Structured logging on every agent action, batch-aggregated to a per-user dashboard. No new LLM call on the hot path.</li>
  <li><strong>Latency budget.</strong> Chip renders inline, under 100ms. Hover panel paints in 400ms.</li>
  <li><strong>Cost ceiling.</strong> Effectively zero — pure metadata. The cost is in storage and a nightly aggregation job.</li>
  <li><strong>Eval.</strong> 50 sample actions per quarter, traced manually from chip back to the underlying LLM call, scored on accuracy of the cost breakdown. Drift on the unit-cost number is the kill metric.</li>
  <li><strong>Two weeks in.</strong> 30% of admin users have opened the dashboard at least once. Renewal-call mentions of "credit spend" go from zero to weekly inside the CSM team.</li>
</ul>

**Feature two: the consumption-expansion card, on the CSM's account record.** When an account's AI-credit usage shows a sustained four-week upward trend crossing a defined threshold, a card surfaces on the CSM's Monday-morning home view — the trend chart, the top three features driving the growth, and a one-line talk track for the expansion conversation. Product telemetry pulls directly into the revenue motion. No reports run, no exports.

<ul class="list-bullet">
  <li><strong>Shape.</strong> Event-stream aggregator running on the weekly batch, plus a single grounded LLM call to render the talk track citing the chart.</li>
  <li><strong>Latency budget.</strong> 30 seconds, async. The card lands before the CSM logs in.</li>
  <li><strong>Cost ceiling.</strong> $0.10 per card. One per account per week, hard-capped.</li>
  <li><strong>Eval.</strong> 80 senior-CSM-reviewed cards, scored on "did this surface an expansion the rep had not already raised in the QBR." Re-run monthly.</li>
  <li><strong>Two weeks in.</strong> 15% of expansion conversations reference at least one fact from the card. If the rate is zero, the threshold is wrong before the talk track is.</li>
</ul>

Both ship in two to three weeks with the team you already have. Both have a kill switch wired to the per-call ceiling. Neither needs a new model contract.
