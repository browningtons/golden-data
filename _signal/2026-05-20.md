ServiceNow put a meter on the AI agent on May 5. Workday and SAP are publishing the same posture. HubSpot shipped outcome-based agent pricing on the May 7 earnings call. Four enterprise vendors moved on the same question inside two weeks: who gets paid when an outside agent does work inside your software.

The seat licence is not the unit any more. The agent action is. ServiceNow's COO said the Action Fabric will "meter" every external agent that touches Now Platform data and bill it through the Assist credits that already price Now Assist. The MCP server is GA. Headless actions cost real credits the moment they cross the boundary.

The bolt-on AI feature your buyer ignored last quarter now sits next to a vendor billing line called "external agent actions." Every CFO with a renewal this year will demand a number for it. The decision in your roadmap is whether your product charges the meter or pays it.

## What's actually moving this week

**1. ServiceNow Action Fabric, Knowledge 2026 (May 5–7).** A control layer between every ServiceNow app and any external AI agent. Pricing shifts from users to activity — customers are charged on how many actions agents perform crossing the fabric. Workday's Agent System of Record is the same architecture in different paint. The next nine months will be vendors publishing "agent action" SKUs. The buyer-side question is whether you build the meter into your own product, or your competitor builds one and routes your agent's actions through theirs.

**2. Trade Desk vs AppLovin, the Q1 split (May 8).** Trade Desk printed $688M, up 12% — pace was 25% YoY this time last year. The stock fell 13%. AppLovin beat-and-raised the same week and popped 6%. Identical demand environment, opposite reactions. Walled gardens are pulling open-web programmatic spend in faster than the deck-slide narrative admits. If your sales motion pitches incrementality on the open web, the next renewal conversation is going to be ugly.

**3. HubSpot Q1 2026 (May 7).** Revenue $881M, up 23%. The note that matters is the pricing shift — outcome-based pricing on Customer and Prospecting Agents, a Customer Agent price cut, 28-day free trials. The CFO said sales cycles will extend while reps re-learn how to sell agents priced on outcomes. HubSpot is the first listed B2B SaaS company to put outcome pricing on an agent SKU in production.

**4. Power BI May 2026 release.** Visual Calculations went GA. Copilot Summarize went GA across every report consumer who clicks it. A new "Prep-Data-For-AI" format standardizes how semantic models declare Copilot readiness. The Tableau move on May 5 was the other half of this picture. Vendors that don't ship the semantic story by Q3 fall off the renewal short list.

**5. Claude for Small Business (May 13).** Anthropic shipped 15 ready-to-run workflows that put Claude inside QuickBooks, PayPal, HubSpot, Canva, DocuSign, Workspace, and Microsoft 365. SMB is the segment where AI defaults already convert without a rep. The agent layer for SMB now ships from Anthropic, surfaced inside other vendors' SKUs. If you sell to SMB, the board question is whether you have a moat against the workflow that runs inside Anthropic's app.

## What I'd ship in your app this week

**Feature one: the agent-action ledger, on the customer admin page.** An IT admin opens settings and sees a panel labelled "AI agent activity in the last 30 days" — every external agent that called your API, what it read, what it changed, the cost of those calls, and a one-click scope-or-revoke button on each agent token. The ledger is the talking point in the renewal meeting before procurement asks for it.

<ul class="list-bullet">
  <li><strong>Shape.</strong> Structured-output extraction over the API access log, joined to agent-token identity. Single grounded call to render the human-readable activity summary; deterministic for the numbers underneath.</li>
  <li><strong>Latency budget.</strong> 8 seconds, lazy-render when the panel opens. The query plan on the access log is the constraint, not the LLM.</li>
  <li><strong>Cost ceiling.</strong> $0.05 per rendered ledger. Per-admin per-day cap. If a tenant scans daily, the deterministic numbers serve from cache and only the summary regenerates.</li>
  <li><strong>Eval.</strong> 150 golden ledgers across 5 agent types — Claude, Copilot Studio, OpenAI Assistants, in-house, partner — scored on "would the IT admin sign off unchanged."</li>
  <li><strong>Two weeks in.</strong> 30%+ of beta admins visit the ledger more than once in their first week. If the count is lower, the panel is in the wrong place on the settings page.</li>
</ul>

**Feature two: the RFP-section drafter, on the ad-platform sales rep's deal record.** A media buyer's RFP includes a clean-room or measurement question the rep can't answer cold. The rep clicks a button on the deal record. An agent assembles a citation-backed draft paragraph from product docs, the last 12 months of answered RFPs, and the legal-approved phrasing library. The rep edits and sends inside the same surface.

<ul class="list-bullet">
  <li><strong>Shape.</strong> Retrieval over RFP corpus + product-docs index + a legal-phrasing rerank step. Single grounded LLM call. Every claim cites the row it was drawn from.</li>
  <li><strong>Latency budget.</strong> 15 seconds. The rep is on the deal record, not in chat. Render in a side panel; the rep keeps scrolling the account while it loads.</li>
  <li><strong>Cost ceiling.</strong> $0.20 per draft. RFP volume is bounded. Per-rep monthly envelope, hard-capped.</li>
  <li><strong>Eval.</strong> 100 senior-AE-and-legal-reviewed drafts, scored on "would this paragraph have shipped to the advertiser as-is." Track the rep edit-rate weekly.</li>
  <li><strong>Two weeks in.</strong> 40% of RFP responses ship with three or fewer rep edits per drafted paragraph. If the edit rate is higher, the retrieval index is wrong before the generation is.</li>
</ul>

Both features ship in two to three weeks with the team you have. Both have a kill switch wired to the cost-per-call ceiling. Neither needs a new model contract.
