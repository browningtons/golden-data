Proof

Example: From dense district PDF → decision-ready degree roadmap

A school district distributed associate degree requirements as a multi-page PDF. Parents and students were expected to interpret credits, categories, constraints, and progress on their own.

The result: confusion, misinterpretation, and repeated clarification conversations.

Before

Static requirements document.
A dense PDF listing courses and categories without a clear way to understand progress or next steps.

After

Decision-ready overview.
Progress, requirements, and constraints visible at a glance.

Supporting view: clear paths forward.
Categories, eligibility, and next steps made explicit — no interpretation required.

Why this worked

  • Progress is visible, not inferred
  • Constraints surface early, not after mistakes
  • The system answers real questions without explanation
  • Everyone is looking at the same source of truth
