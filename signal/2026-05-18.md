ServiceNow opened its full system of action to any AI agent on May 5. Walmart Connect launched an agentic advertiser assistant the same week. Outreach announced its ServiceNow CRM integration the day after. Read the three announcements together. Then read Tableau's Open Semantic Interchange and Snowflake's MCP-callable Cortex Agents from the last fortnight. The pattern is the same.

The agent stopped being the moat. Vendors are competing on the substrate the agent runs on — the data layer, the action fabric, the governance shell. ServiceNow's framing was the cleanest: Claude calls into us, Copilot calls into us, your homegrown agent calls into us, and we are where the work actually happens. The agent layer is commoditizing. The control plane is the seat being defended.

For founders building in this space, the strategic question shifted in three weeks. It is no longer "what is our agent." It is "whose agents call ours, and whose substrate are we callable on top of."

## What's actually moving this week

**1. ServiceNow opened its platform to any agent.** Knowledge 2026 in early May shipped Action Fabric — an MCP server that exposes ServiceNow's full set of governed enterprise actions to any agent, whether built on ServiceNow or with Claude, Copilot, or a customer's own stack. AI Control Tower expansions hit Innovation Lab with August GA. The framing is the news. ServiceNow stopped pitching its agent. Started pitching the substrate every agent runs through.

**2. Walmart Connect shipped an agentic advertiser assistant.** Walmart Q1 2026 printed May 15. Connect grew 31% in the US ex-VIZIO and global ad revenue surged 50% with VIZIO included. The agentic assistant — conversational chat that builds, optimizes, and troubleshoots campaigns — is now the SMB onboarding surface for the third-largest ad business in the country. The retail-media-as-conversational-UI thesis just became a thing advertiser-tool RFPs have to answer.

**3. Outreach plugged into ServiceNow.** Announced May 6. Revenue orchestration moves into the CRM workspace. The previous decade of sales-tool sprawl — Outreach over here, the CRM over there, the call recorder somewhere else — is collapsing into one surface where the action and the system of record are the same thing. The next renewal cycle that touches a sales-engagement seat will have a "why not just stay inside ServiceNow" slide in the procurement deck.

**4. The AI churn wave got real numbers.** SaaStr published horizontal-AI-agent retention data this week — roughly 40% median gross retention, with buyers explicitly refusing multi-year contracts because prompts are portable and frontier models keep moving. The implication for product strategy is sharp. Durable AI revenue lives where it grounds on proprietary data the buyer cannot replicate by lifting your prompt template. The substrate is the moat. Again.

## What I'd ship in your app this week

**Feature one: the agent ledger, on the customer admin console.** Every customer's admin opens a single page and sees every AI call your product made on their behalf in the last 90 days. Who triggered it. What data it grounded on. What action it took. What it cost them. When their procurement review hits, they screenshot the ledger and walk into the renewal meeting with the line item their CFO was about to ask about anyway. You just gave the buyer the artifact they needed to defend the seat.

<ul class="list-bullet">
  <li><strong>Shape.</strong> Append-only event log per tenant. Single grounded LLM call to summarize at view time. CSV export from day one.</li>
  <li><strong>Latency budget.</strong> 3 seconds, lazy paint on page open. Not real-time during agent execution.</li>
  <li><strong>Cost ceiling.</strong> $0.05 per page render. Most admins never look. Hard cap per tenant per day.</li>
  <li><strong>Eval.</strong> 150 golden ledger rows lifted from real customer support escalations, scored on "would the buyer file a procurement objection after reading this row." Run weekly.</li>
  <li><strong>Two weeks in.</strong> 30% of accounts in the rollout cohort export the ledger at least once. If they don't, the page is in the wrong place in the admin nav.</li>
</ul>

**Feature two: the substrate-fluent onboarding flow, on the SMB advertiser self-serve.** First-time advertiser logs in. A co-pilot asks three questions about the business. Drafts a 7-day starter campaign grounded on comparable advertisers in the same vertical and geo. Shows the budget with one citation: "based on N similar advertisers, $X/day hits CPA under Y in your sector." Every input the advertiser changes triggers a one-call re-explanation.

<ul class="list-bullet">
  <li><strong>Shape.</strong> Retrieval-augmented single call. Lookalike index built offline from the last 90 days of platform spend. Re-rank on geo and vertical.</li>
  <li><strong>Latency budget.</strong> 5 seconds first draft. 2 seconds on iteration.</li>
  <li><strong>Cost ceiling.</strong> $0.10 per onboarding session. Hard cap per advertiser per week.</li>
  <li><strong>Eval.</strong> 100 golden sessions scored by senior SMB account managers on "would I have recommended the same campaign shape."</li>
  <li><strong>Two weeks in.</strong> 25% lift in seven-day completed-campaign rate on the SMB cohort against a holdout. If you don't see it, the lookalike index is too narrow.</li>
</ul>

Both features ship in two to three weeks with the team you already have. Both have a kill switch wired to the cost ceiling. Neither needs a model contract you don't already have.
